About

Mauritius — my parents' home, and the place that has always complicated simple questions about where I'm from.

Raised brown in Singapore, a native French and Mandarin speaker, making it easy to upset anyone trying to categorize me. NRIC reads 'MAURITIAN'. The bulk of my youth was spent translating: languages, codes, different versions of me. Hence, the work ends up in the same place: problems for people the system wasn't designed for.

OTR research relied on emotion theory, linguistics and ML; Reach Alliance kept revealing stuff that would have been hidden in the default system design — collectivist decision-making, preference for non-verbal signals. No good name for that sort of work. Some midpoint between technical and ethnographic.

Before these projects, co-founded Kura, a voice journaling app: 5 years, 2,500 users, $50k funding. Shut down last year and am still wrestling with the implications of the experience.

Every project hit the same obstacle: technical problems, sure, but beyond that lay unaddressed ones: who is this tool for, what assumptions does it make about the user. I have no new tools to offer answers to these questions.

Presently, in trade & economic policy. Soon, possibly, into law. I read a mix of economics, philosophy, and whatever is grabbing my attention at the moment — currently some combination of Nagarjuna and structural gravity models. Preparing for a half marathon. Making slow but steady progress toward DELF B2 in French and beginner level Portuguese.

You can reach me at arifwoozeer@gmail.com or via LinkedIn, GitHub, and Goodreads.
