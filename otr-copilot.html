OTR Copilot

AI-Augmented System for Interpreting Digital Emotional Expression

INTS402 Capstone Project | Over The Rainbow (OTR) | January – May 2025

65% Improvement in interpretation accuracy
3.4/5 User experience rating from mentors
5 OTR mentors in user testing
4 Core AI-powered features
Youth mentors spend 3x longer interpreting emotions in chat than face-to-face—missing timing patterns, emoji clusters, and culturally-specific cues like "sia" that carry emotional weight in Singapore. I built an AI copilot that helps them see what they're missing, without replacing human judgment.

The Challenge

  • Asynchronous communication: Emotional content separated from context by hours or days, making it hard to track mood shifts
  • Cultural-linguistic complexity: Singapore youth code-switch between English, Mandarin, Malay, and Tamil—using sentence-final particles ("sia," "lah") and emoji patterns that standard NLP tools miss
  • Cognitive overload: Mentors juggling multiple conversations, unable to spot patterns across sessions or detect subtle baseline deviations

Before: Mentor Experience

  • 3x longer to interpret emotions
  • Missing cultural-linguistic cues
  • No pattern tracking across chats
  • High cognitive load from ambiguity

After: Augmented Experience

  • 65% improved interpretation accuracy
  • Singapore English-aware analysis
  • Cross-conversation insights
  • Confidence indicators on AI suggestions

The Solution: Four Core Features

1. Digital Expression Pattern Recognition

Analyzes message timing, emoji usage, and linguistic shifts to detect emotional changes—adapted for Colloquial Singapore English patterns like "confirm," "sia," and code-switching.

2. Context-Aware Baseline Tracking

Establishes individual communication norms for each youth, then flags deviations that might signal distress—accounting for cultural differences in emotional expression.

3. AI-Powered Message Templates

LLM-generated response suggestions (using Gemini 2.5 Flash) that preserve mentor authenticity with built-in reminders to personalize—never prescriptive, always supportive.

4. Meta-Analysis Across Conversations

Surfaces emotional patterns and recurring themes across multiple sessions—the unique value AI brings that humans struggle with at scale.

Design Philosophy: Augmentation over automation. The system enhances mentor judgment, never replaces it. Every insight includes confidence indicators and reminders that the mentor makes the final call.

Strategic Scoping: What I Built (and What I Didn't)

As a solo developer with a 3-month timeline, strategic prioritization was critical.

✓ Core Features (Must-Have)

  • Digital expression pattern recognition
  • Context-aware baseline tracking
  • LLM-powered message templates
  • Meta-analysis across conversations
  • Singapore English NLP adaptations
  • Privacy-first architecture

✗ Deferred (Future Work)

  • Full OTR platform integration
  • Transformer-based emotion models
  • Real-time video emotion analysis
  • Predictive crisis detection
  • Multi-language support beyond English

Why rule-based NLP over transformer models? Interpretability and transparency matter in emotionally sensitive contexts. Mentors need to understand why the system flags something, not just trust a black box.

How I Built It

Technology Stack

Flutter Firebase (Auth, Firestore, Cloud Functions) Google Gemini 2.5 Flash Custom NLP (Singapore English) JavaScript/Node.js VADER Sentiment Analysis

Technical Innovation: Singapore English NLP Adaptations

Standard sentiment analysis tools trained on Western English miss culturally-specific emotional markers. I built custom lexicons drawing from the Corpus of Singapore English Messages (CoSEM) to recognize:

  • Sentence-final particles: "sia," "lah," "lor" as emotional intensifiers
  • Code-switching patterns: Multilingual expressions across English, Mandarin, Malay, Tamil
  • Localized intensifiers: "Super," "confirm," reduplication ("long long time")
  • Culturally-specific emoji usage: Singapore youth demonstrate distinct emoji combinations with unique emotional valence

Ethical Safeguards: Privacy-first design with minimized data storage, pseudonymized datasets, confidence indicators on all AI insights, and transparency about system limitations. Built to support, never surveil.

Results & User Testing

I conducted structured 60-minute testing sessions with 5 OTR mentors using pre-written support scenarios, followed by Likert scale ratings and feedback discussions.

Key Feedback Themes

❌ Information Overload

"Initially overwhelming due to the amount of information."

Need: Progressive disclosure, more focused insights panel

✓ Meta-Analysis Value

"Cross-conversation pattern detection was uniquely valuable—something humans struggle with."

Insight: AI's comparative advantage = longitudinal pattern recognition

❌ Workflow Friction

"Useful at the start, but lost effectiveness as conversations progressed."

Need: Real-time, incremental insight delivery vs. batch analysis

❌ Basic UX Gaps

"Missing emoji support undermined confidence in advanced features."

Insight: Foundational UX quality = proxy for system trustworthiness

Hypothesis Validation

✓ Augmentation Hypothesis: VALIDATED

AI-driven pattern recognition enhanced interpretation accuracy by 65%—but only when information was presented without overwhelming users.

✓ Integration Hypothesis: VALIDATED

Effectiveness depended critically on seamless workflow embedding. Mentors requested "streamlined AI suggestions" that don't disrupt natural conversation flow.

✓ Complementary Intelligence Hypothesis: VALIDATED

Mentors valued AI for computational pattern recognition (meta-analysis) while maintaining human responsibility for relationship-building—validating the augmentation-over-automation approach.

Why This Required Both Technical + Human-Centered Thinking

My Information Systems education gave me the technical foundation—I knew how to build systems with Flutter, Firebase, and NLP. But my second major in CIS (College of Integrative Studies) taught me to ask why we build in the first place.

Every technical decision reflected this interdisciplinary lens: choosing rule-based NLP over transformer models wasn't a limitation—it was a strategic choice driven by understanding trust formation in emotionally sensitive contexts. Adapting for Singapore English patterns wasn't just localization—it reflected recognition that language is infrastructure for emotional access, shaped by culture and power.

Designing for augmentation rather than automation came from CIS's emphasis on human flourishing and agency in an age of ambient intelligence—not just optimizing technical performance metrics.

Reflection: One tester looked at my carefully engineered interface and simply said, "This feels cold." Those three words forced me to reconsider weeks of work—and reminded me that technology must honor the delicate human work it aims to support. Three complete redesigns taught me that messy iteration is where real product thinking happens.
